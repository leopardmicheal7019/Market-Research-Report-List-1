<p><p>Black Silicon Carbide (SiC) is a hard, crystalline material used primarily in industrial applications. The Metallurgical Grade Black SiC is utilized mainly as a deoxidizer in steel production and as an abrasive due to its high hardness. In contrast, the Refractory Grade Black SiC is employed in high-temperature applications, such as in the manufacture of kiln furniture and furnaces, where resistance to heat and thermal shock is essential. Both grades serve distinct markets based on their physical properties and applications.</p></p>
