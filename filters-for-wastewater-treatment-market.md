<p><p>The wastewater treatment market features various filtration technologies, prominently including granular media filtration and fiber filtration. Granular media filtration utilizes layers of granular materials, such as sand or gravel, to trap solid particles and impurities from wastewater. This method is effective for large volumes and can handle varying flow rates. Conversely, fiber filtration employs synthetic or natural fibers to create a dense mat that captures fine particles and biological contaminants, offering higher filtration efficiency and improved water quality. Both types play essential roles in enhancing the treatment process.</p></p>
