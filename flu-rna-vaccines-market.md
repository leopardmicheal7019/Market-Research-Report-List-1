<p><p>The Flu RNA Vaccines market includes several types based on their mechanisms. Non-replicating mRNA vaccines deliver genetic material that instructs cells to produce viral proteins, generating an immune response without replicating the mRNA. In vivo self-replicating mRNA vaccines enhance immunity by allowing the mRNA to replicate inside cells, boosting protein production. In vitro dendritic cell non-replicating mRNA vaccines involve extracting dendritic cells, which are then loaded with mRNA to stimulate an immune response. Other market types may include novel approaches and hybrid technologies.</p></p>
