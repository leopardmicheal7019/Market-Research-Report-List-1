<p><p>The injectable thyroid drug market primarily comprises two types: Levothyroxine Sodium and Liothyronine Sodium. Levothyroxine Sodium is a synthetic form of the thyroid hormone thyroxine (T4), commonly used to treat hypothyroidism by restoring normal hormone levels in the body. Liothyronine Sodium, on the other hand, is a synthetic form of triiodothyronine (T3), offering quicker action for patients with severe thyroid hormone deficiencies. Both products play crucial roles in managing thyroid-related disorders, enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
