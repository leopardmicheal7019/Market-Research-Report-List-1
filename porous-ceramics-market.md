<p><p>The porous ceramics market can be categorized into two primary types: oxides and non-oxides ceramics. Oxide ceramics, primarily made from aluminum oxide and silica, are known for their high thermal stability, chemical resistance, and durability, making them ideal for applications like filtration and catalysts. Non-oxide ceramics, including carbides, nitrides, and borides, offer superior mechanical properties and thermal resistance, often used in advanced applications such as aerospace and electronics. Both types play crucial roles in diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
