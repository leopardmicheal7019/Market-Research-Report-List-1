<p><p>Residual Solvent Analysis is essential in ensuring the safety and quality of pharmaceuticals and other products. The market is primarily categorized into two types: Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC is a widely used technique for separating and quantifying volatile residual solvents, providing quick results. In contrast, GC-MS combines GC with mass spectrometry for enhanced sensitivity and specificity, allowing for the identification of complex solvent mixtures. Together, these methods ensure compliance with safety standards.</p></p>
