<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is a technology used in diesel commercial vehicles to reduce nitrogen oxides (NOx) emissions. The key types include NH3-SCR, Urea-SCR, and Ammonia-SCR. NH3-SCR uses ammonia directly as a reducing agent, while Urea-SCR injects urea solution (Diesel Exhaust Fluid) into the exhaust system to convert NOx into harmless nitrogen and water. Ammonia-SCR encompasses both methods, utilizing ammonia, either directly or via urea, to enhance emissions control efficiency and meet environmental regulations.</p></p>
