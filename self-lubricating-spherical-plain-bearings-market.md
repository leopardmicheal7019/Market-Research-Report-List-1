<p><p>The self-lubricating spherical plain bearings market comprises two main types: metallic and non-metallic self-lubricated bearings. Metallic self-lubricated bearings typically use special alloys or coatings that minimize friction and wear while providing durability under heavy loads. In contrast, non-metallic self-lubricated bearings, often made from polymers or composites, offer lightweight, corrosion-resistant options suitable for various environments. Both types eliminate the need for external lubrication, making them ideal for applications where maintenance is challenging or impractical, enhancing overall efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
