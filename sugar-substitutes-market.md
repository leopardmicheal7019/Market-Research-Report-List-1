<p><p>The sugar substitutes market comprises three key types: high-intensity sweeteners, low-intensity sweeteners, and high fructose syrup. Low-intensity sweeteners, like sugar alcohols, offer a milder sweetness with fewer calories but can cause digestive issues in some. High fructose syrup, derived from corn, is a calorie-dense sweetener commonly used in processed foods, linking to concerns about obesity and metabolic health. Together, these substitutes cater to diverse dietary preferences and needs.</p></p>
