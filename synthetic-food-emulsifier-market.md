<p><p>The synthetic food emulsifier market is categorized into several types, including Span, Tween, Glyceryl Monostearate, and others. Span emulsifiers, also known as sorbitan esters, are primarily used in food products for their ability to stabilize oil-in-water emulsions. Tween emulsifiers, or polyoxyethylene sorbitan esters, enhance solubility and texture. Glyceryl Monostearate, a widely used emulsifier, aids in improving the consistency and shelf life of various food items. The "Other" category encompasses alternative emulsifiers with unique applications in food processing.</p></p>
