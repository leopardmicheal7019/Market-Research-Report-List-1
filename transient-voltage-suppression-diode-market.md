<p><p>The transient-voltage-suppression (TVS) diode market is categorized into uni-polar and bi-polar TVS types. Uni-polar TVS diodes are designed to clamp voltage spikes in one direction, making them ideal for circuits where the voltage polarity is always positive. In contrast, bi-polar TVS diodes can handle voltage transients in both directions, providing protection for circuits that may experience voltage fluctuations in either polarity. These distinctions influence their applications in various electronic devices, ensuring effective surge protection in diverse environments.</p></p>
