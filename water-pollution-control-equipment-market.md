<p><p>The water pollution control equipment market is divided into two primary types: particulate contaminants and chemical contaminants. Particulate contaminant control involves equipment designed to remove solid particles from water, such as sedimentation tanks, filters, and centrifuges. In contrast, chemical contaminant control focuses on the treatment of dissolved substances, employing technologies like chemical dosing systems, advanced oxidation processes, and ion exchange units. Both types of equipment play crucial roles in ensuring water quality and compliance with environmental regulations.</p></p>
